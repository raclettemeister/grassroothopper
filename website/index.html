01

Who are Grassroot Hoppers?

Hopping across open-source projects to build what communities actually need.

All over the world, people are building open-source alternatives to the platforms that run our lives and make billions selling our attention. Cooperative delivery apps. Community-owned music streaming. Federated social networks. Open-source event tools.

Grassroot hopping is the act of taking the best of what exists, co-building what's needed, and leading, advocating, and driving even more open source alternatives. Then assembling it into something communities can use and own.

Not building everything from scratch — catching everything that's being built. Hopping from grassroots repos to grassroots repos.

02

The first idea: a local social network owned by its members.

Imagine a social network for your city — but without the corporation behind it. No ads, no algorithm, no data harvesting. Just the creative people in your neighborhood and the events happening around you.

No noise, no complaining. It's about art, creativity, classes, common hobbies, projects to join.

07

How a community works.

→

Someone starts it. A dancer, a gallery owner, a yoga teacher, a group of friends. They become the admin. They create a QR code and choose the first secret password.

→

People join in person. At an event, a class, a meetup. Scan the code, say the password, pick a username. No app store, no sign-up form — the whole thing runs in your browser.

→

Events are the gravity. The community calendar becomes the single source of truth for “what's happening this week.” People come back for the events. They discover the creators along the way.

→

The community governs itself. Members vote on decisions — password changes, new features, how things are run. The admin facilitates, but the community owns it.

→

Communities overlap. A dancer is also in the jazz community. An artist is in three neighborhoods. People bridge communities naturally. The web grows through real human connections.

The cascade
→
No comments means no comment moderation, no harassment tools, no abuse reporting, no content ranking.
→
No DMs means no spam filters, no blocking system, no encryption infrastructure, no predator detection.
→
No likes means no engagement metrics, no dopamine loops, no content ranking algorithms, no vanity economy.
→
No payments means no PCI compliance, no financial liability, no transaction disputes, no regulatory burden.
→
What's left? Blogs behind a gate + an event calendar. A solved problem. The entire backend complexity of a social platform disappears.
→
Simple enough that a bounty developer can build a feature in a weekend. Simple enough that any city can run it. Simple enough that it's sustainable on €5/month creator fees.

Subtraction is the product strategy.

Every feature removed makes the platform cheaper to run, easier to fork, harder to abuse, and more focused on what actually matters: real people discovering each other's creative lives.
10

You're not a user.
You're a co-owner.

The cooperative funds itself, governs itself, and improves itself. No investors. No ads. Just members.

1
Members join through their communities.
Scan a QR code, say the password. Choose reader or creator. You're in — and you're a co-owner. Your name goes on the cooperative register.
2
Creators pay €5/month. Readers are free.
200 creators = €1,000/month. That covers hosting, domains, and dev bounties. The co-op is self-funding from day one if the community is real.
3
Members vote. Weighted by role.
Creators, event planners, readers, devs — each class votes with different weight. Decisions are made through open-source governance tools.
4
The co-op improves the platform.
Members decide what gets built. Bounties are posted for developers. The platform gets better because the people who use it are the people who own it.
5
Better platform attracts more communities.
Word spreads. More communities want in. More members means more revenue, more voices, better decisions. The cycle continues.
6
Every improvement benefits every member.
Because you co-own it, every feature built, every community added, every tool integrated — it all comes back to you. That's not a marketing line. It's a legal fact.
the cycle repeats — stronger each time
Voting power by class
Creators~40% · €5/mo · the heart of the platform
Event Planners~30% · scaled fee · they bring the gravity
Readers~20% · free · the audience every creator needs
Devs~10% · free · their contribution is the code
“I'm a co-owner of the social network I use.”
That sentence doesn't exist anywhere else. It's a legal fact. Your name is on the register. You vote. You share in the value. CoopCycle proved this model works for delivery. This is the same model for creative communities.
